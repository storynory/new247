bio: |-
  Professor Heather Widdows is the John Ferguson Professor of Global Ethics and the Deputy Pro-Vice Chancellor for Research Impact at the University of Birmingham. In this role she seeks to support and extend the impact of Birmingham's research across policy, cultural and industrial sectors.  

  She has served as a member of the UK Biobank Ethics and Governance Council (2007-2013), the Philosophy REF-panel (2013-2014) and she is currently a member of the Nuffield Council on Bioethics. She has run multidisciplinary grants on Property Regulation in European Science, Ethics and Law and on the ethics and governance of human tissue. She has collaborated on grants on terrorism and trust and is currently working on the increasing demands of beauty.
