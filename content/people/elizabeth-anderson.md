bio: |-
  Elizabeth Anderson is Professor and John Dewey Distinguished University Professor of Philosophy and Women's Studies.  She teaches courses  in ethics, social and political philosophy, political economy, philosophy of the social sciences, and feminist theory.

  Her research focuses on democratic theory, equality in political philosophy and American law, racial integration, the ethical limits of markets, theories of value
  and rational choice (alternatives to consequentialism and economic theories of rational choice), the philosophies of John Stuart Mill and John Dewey, social epistemology,
  and feminist epistemology and philosophy of science.  

  She is currently working on the history of egalitarianism, with a special focus on the social epistemology of
  moral learning, taking the history of abolitionism as a central case study.  she is designed and was the first Director of the Program in Philosophy, Politics, and
  Economics.
